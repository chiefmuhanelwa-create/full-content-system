## FRAMEWORK 1 — The 4 Foundational Scripting Principles

**Apply to every line of every script. No exceptions.**

### Principle 1: Negativity Always Wins
**Rule:** Negativity invokes more attention and emotion than positivity. Use it — but only indirectly.
**Do:** Attack the problem, the system, the mistake the viewer is making.
**Never do:** Attack the viewer directly. "You're lazy" is an attack. "This mistake is keeping you broke" is strategy.

**Examples:**
- ❌ "You're making this mistake" → personal attack
- ✅ "This mistake is costing you R50K/year" → attack the mistake, not the person
- ❌ "You don't have a system" → shame
- ✅ "Content without a system is just free advertising for brands" → attack the situation

**Stories that demonstrate this principle:**
- Burnout story: "Consistency without strategy is just exhausting" — attacks the approach, not the creator
- SARS story: "Nobody teaches creators about tax" — attacks the education gap, not the creator

---

### Principle 2: You Format
**Rule:** Every sentence speaks directly to one person. Never use "they", "people", "someone", "one", "folks", "everyone".

**Replacements:**
- "People often struggle with..." → "You struggle with..."
- "Most creators don't know..." → "You probably don't know..."
- "Someone in your position should..." → "You should..."
- "They need to build..." → "You need to build..."

**Why it works:** The viewer doesn't feel like a statistic. They feel like the content was written for them. That's the difference between passive scrolling and stopping.

**Test:** Read the line aloud. If it sounds like you're talking TO one person sitting across from you — it passes.

---

### Principle 3: Short & Simple
**Rule:** Ruthless brevity. One idea per sentence. Active voice. Cut every filler word.

**Cut list:**
- "just" (almost always unnecessary)
- "really" (show it, don't emphasise it)
- "very" (use a stronger word instead)
- "actually" (start with what's actual)
- "literally" (overused)
- "in order to" → "to"
- "the fact that" → cut it
- "at this point in time" → "now"

**Structure:**
- Short sentence. Then a slightly longer one that explains. Then a short gut-punch. Repeat.
- Read it aloud at the speed you'll deliver it. If you run out of breath mid-sentence, cut it.

---

### Principle 4: Audible Flow
**Rule:** Every line must sound natural when read aloud. If you stumble — rewrite.

**Test:** Read the script aloud at delivery speed. Mark every place you stumble, pause unnaturally, or lose breath. Rewrite those lines.

**Markers that improve flow:**
- [PAUSE] — deliberate pause for emphasis
- Bold a word you want to emphasise vocally
- Short sentences before a beat = natural pause built in

**Anti-patterns:**
- Long dependent clauses before the main verb
- Passive voice (hard to deliver naturally)
- Jargon that slows reading
- Sentences that require a breath mid-way through

## FRAMEWORK 2 — R×A×C×U^B Hook Formula

**The formula:** `R × A × C × U^B`

### What Each Letter Means

**R — Relevant**
Who exactly is this for? The hook must speak to a specific person's specific situation.
- Generic: "Want to grow your social media?"
- Relevant: "You have 50,000 followers and can't pay rent."
The more specific, the more the right person feels like you read their mind.

**A — Aware**
What level of problem awareness does your audience have?
| Awareness Level | What They Know | Hook Style |
|----------------|---------------|------------|
| Symptom Aware | "Something's wrong but I don't know what" | Name the symptom: "Your content isn't working..." |
| Problem Aware | "I know my problem but not the solution" | Name the problem: "You're posting without a system..." |
| Solution Aware | "I know what I need but not who" | Differentiate: "Most monetisation courses teach the wrong metric..." |
| Product Aware | "I know your product" | Offer: "Here's what's inside the PAIDS course..." |

**C — Clear Outcome**
What exactly will they get? The outcome must be specific and believable.
- Vague: "Learn how to make money from content"
- Clear: "5 income streams from your existing content. Set up in 30 days."

**Four Hook Types (Only These Four — Not Question/Story/Statement):**
1. **Information Gap** — "Here's what brands never tell creators about pricing."
2. **Desired Result** — "How to turn one brand deal into 18 months of retainer income."
3. **Undesired Result** — "What saying yes to R2,500 deals is actually costing you."
4. **A-to-B Transformation** — "From 0 brand deals to R100K — here's the exact path."

**U^B — Unique^Broadened**
U = Pattern interrupt. Does something unexpected. Breaks the scroll.
B = Still accessible to a wide audience. Not so niche only 3 people understand it.

The power: `U^B` is the multiplier. Good R×A×C = decent hook. Add U^B = viral hook.

### Hook Formula by Audience

**For The Called Expert:**
- Awareness: Problem aware (they know they're invisible, not why)
- Hook type: Information Gap or A-to-B
- Example: "You have 10 years of expertise. The person with 100K followers who knows less charges 10x more. Here's why."

**For The Content Creator Inspirer:**
- Awareness: Symptom aware → Problem aware (they feel it but can't name the system problem)
- Hook type: Undesired Result or A-to-B
- Example: "You've been posting for a year. Same follower count. Same bank balance. This is why."

## FRAMEWORK 3 — 5 Story Types

**The 5 types of proof story every content creator needs in their library.**

### Type 1: Origin Story
**What it is:** Where you started. Why you do this. The before that makes the after mean something.
**When to use:** Building a new audience | First impressions | About page content | Brand pitch
**Emotion:** Vulnerability → Purpose
**Structure:** Before (the struggle) → The moment (what changed) → Now (what's possible)

**Ndivhuwo's Origin Story:** Bathroom floors → Building from nothing → "This is why every framework has a receipt."

**Products to mention:** Any product. This is credibility, not a pitch.

---

### Type 2: Struggle Story
**What it is:** The dark moment that created the lesson. Not the success — the failure.
**When to use:** When you want deep empathy | When your audience is going through something hard | Motivation content
**Emotion:** Shame/Pain → Lesson → Hope
**Structure:** The moment it hit hardest → Why it hurt → What it taught → What's different now

**Ndivhuwo's Struggle Stories:**
- SARS R285K — the cost of financial ignorance
- Family shame — the cost of unconventional choices
- Content burnout — the cost of volume without system

**Products to mention:** Tax product, burnout → system products (PAIDS course)

---

### Type 3: Transformation Story
**What it is:** The before/after with your method as the bridge. Proof that it works.
**When to use:** Selling | Social proof | Demonstrating the system | Launch content
**Emotion:** Doubt/Stuck → Discovery → Transformation
**Structure:** Where I/they were → The method I found → Where I/they are now → You can do this too

**Ndivhuwo's Transformation Stories:**
- R750 → R100K: The method = learn your value, not your desperation
- Content burnout → R50K: The method = system over schedule
- 6 months/R0 → R50K/month: The method = one strategic post over daily noise

**Products to mention:** PAIDS Starter Course, brand deal products (03, 04, 05)

---

### Type 4: Breakthrough Story
**What it is:** The 'aha' moment — the insight that changed everything. Teaching through discovery.
**When to use:** Educational content | Framework introduction | Teaching moments
**Emotion:** Confusion → Discovery → Clarity
**Structure:** The question that bothered me → What I did to find the answer → The insight → Why it matters to you

**Ndivhuwo's Breakthrough Stories:**
- Netflix deal: The insight = brands buy engagement rates, not follower counts
- Samsung: The insight = ask about business goals, not content goals
- R6K Huawei: The insight = investment mindset > waiting-for-perfect mindset

**Products to mention:** Product 13 (be ready), Product 03 (brand DM response), PAIDS Pro

---

### Type 5: Lesson Story
**What it is:** What you wish you knew. Preventing their mistakes through your experience.
**When to use:** Educational content | Warning content | Tax/legal/financial content
**Emotion:** Empathy (I've been there) → Education (here's what I know now)
**Structure:** Here's the mistake → Here's the cost → Here's what to do instead

**Ndivhuwo's Lesson Stories:**
- SARS R285K: "I wish someone had told me brand income is taxable from the first rand."
- R750 undercharging: "I wish someone had told me that agencies have rate databases."

## FRAMEWORK 4 — 7-Stage Story Arc

**Every story that lands uses this arc. Even unconsciously.**

| Stage | Name | What Happens | SA Story Example |
|-------|------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Normal World | Life before the disruption — relatable baseline | "I was posting every day, brand deals trickling in, thinking this was it." |
| 2 | Disruption | The event that changes everything | "Then the Samsung brief came in. The biggest budget I'd ever seen." |
| 3 | Resistance | Internal doubt, external obstacle, the hesitation | "I almost said my usual rate. R15K. One post. Done." |
| 4 | Crisis Point | The decision point — where everything hangs | "They asked for my retainer rate. I had never charged a retainer in my life." |
| 5 | Decision | The choice made, usually at great risk | "I quoted R450K for 18 months. Silence on the other end." |
| 6 | Transformation | What happened as a result | "They agreed. That one decision changed how I built my entire business." |
| 7 | New World | The life after — what's now possible | "Now every client conversation starts with retainer rate. Not one-off rate." |

### Duration Adaptations

**For 15-60s content (Reels/TikTok):**
Start at Stage 4 (Crisis Point). Compress 4→5→6. End with 7 as the lesson.
"I had to quote a retainer rate with zero experience. I said R450K. They said yes. Here's what that taught me."

**For 3-10 minute content (YouTube, Podcast):**
Use all 7 stages. Spend most time on 3 (Resistance) and 5 (Decision). The struggle is the story.

**For long-form written content (LinkedIn, Email):**
Use all 7 stages with full detail. Add numbers, emotions, context at every stage.

**Core thesis:** Platforms are rented rivers. Your community is an owned tank. Fish die when rivers dry up.

| Element | Meaning | Who controls it |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| River | Social platforms (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube) | The platform |
| Fish | Your audience | Nobody — they're free |
| Bait | Your content | You |
| Tank | Email list, community, owned platform | You |

**The 4-Stage Content Formula:**
1. **Hook** — cast the bait, stop the scroll
2. **Story** — reel them in with 5 Story Types arc
3. **Lesson** — deliver the framework teaching

**The 4 Contentpreneur Levels:**
- Level 1: Creator (no income, expression only)
- Level 2: Platform Dependent (brand deals, Adsense — risky)
- Level 3: Platform Independent (email list, digital products — owns tank)

## FRAMEWORK 15 — Global CPM Strategy

**Source:** Contentpreneur book, Chapter 10

## FRAMEWORK 18 — 80/20 Content Principle

**Source:** Contentpreneur book, Chapter 7 — "Burnout And Broke"  
**Discovery:** Out of 600 pieces/month (2019–2022), only ~20 actually drove income. 97% of effort → 20% of results.

**The 20% (strategic content that works):**
- Framework teaching (complete system)
- Proof content (before/after with specific numbers)
- Polarising positions (clear point of view that invites reaction)
- Problem-specific content (one urgent pain, one solution)
- Transformation stories (emotional arc + credible result)

**The 80% (algorithm food, not business food):**
- Trend-chasing without niche relevance
- Generic motivation without proof
- Quota-filling content with no specific goal
- Vague topics applicable to everyone = relevant to no one

**The shift:** Activity-focused (95% creating, 5% building) → Asset-focused (30% creating, 50% building, 20% managing systems)
